#English #words 

# admonition

* warning to sb about their behaviour 警告；告诫
* admonitionment 也是一个意思，但是使用不频繁

>Still, Mr. Obama is expected to pair his calls for investment with an **admonition** that the country must embark on targeted spending cuts.
>
>不过，预计奥巴马在呼吁投资的同时，还会告诫美国必须开始有针对性地削减开支。

# admonish

* **admonish somebody (for something/for doing something)** to tell somebody strongly and clearly that you do not approve of something that they have done 警告，批评

>She was admonished for chewing gum in class.
>
>她因为在课堂上嚼口香糖而被警告。

* **admonish somebody (to do something)** to strongly advise somebody to do something 警告，告诫

>A warning voice admonished him not to let this happen.
>
>一个警醒的声音告诫他不要让这种事发生。

* *注意上面两种意思的区别！*

# admonish 和 warn

* admonish 一般指年长者或领导对已犯错误的或有过失的人提出的忠告或警告以避免类似错误。
* warn 含义与 caution 相同，但语气较重，尤指重后果。

# admonitory

* [adjective] that is a warning to somebody about their behaviour 劝告的，警告的

>The sergeant lifted an admonitory finger at us.
>
>中士警告式地用手指指着我们。
